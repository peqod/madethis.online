CopyTo, simple, easy, free, user (and cli ;) first web clipper.

copyto saves quotes, images, and clean article copies as portable Markdown in a folder the user chose. There is no account, cloud storage, analytics service, or remote processing in the clipping path. The files remain readable without CopyTo. But with Copyto they are easily accessible through a neat feed.

## The opportunity

Most reading tools create a dependency at the moment they promise convenience. Saved material enters somebody else's database, export quality becomes a policy choice, and the reader's own annotations are trapped beside copyrighted source material they do not own.

CopyTo separates those concerns. Private source material stays local. The reader's commentary is stored distinctly and can later become the basis of an opt-in publishing product. That boundary makes a future network possible without quietly turning a private library into platform inventory.

## Product direction

The local product is deliberately narrow: reliable capture, useful daily notes, an offline reader, and files that Obsidian or another tool can index. Stable identifiers and publication-ready metadata are written early so existing vaults will not require a destructive migration later.

The longer-term value is a publishing surface built around commentary rather than copied articles: a durable bridge between reading, thinking, and sharing, with user ownership preserved at every step.
